Systems Administrators configure and manage computer systems in a networked setting. They oversee the installation and ongoing management of networks of desktop computers, wireless networks, server systems and other critical networks in a business.

Salary range: $90,000 to $110,000.

Report to: IT Manager

Who We're Seeking:

Above all, we're seeking someone with robust project-based experience and a knack for designing and implementing IT Infrastructure solutions. Exceptional customer service skills are non-negotiable, and you should thrive both independently and within a team. We're in search of a visionary who champions innovation and is not afraid to explore unconventional avenues!

Key responsibilities:

  1. Configuring, maintaining and controlling computer networks.
  2. Troubleshooting technical network and server problems and providing technical support.
  3. Diagnosing, resolving and documenting server and network issues.
  4. Developing and maintaining network records and documentation.

Preferable skill

  • Demonstrated experience in cybersecurity and monitoring services

About this position

A user experience (UX) engineer builds and designs digital products like websites and apps while using UX principles. UX engineers generally deal with the front end of these products—the user-facing parts that people interact with, like the buttons, layouts, and flow of the interfaces. They may work on both engineering teams or design teams, or act as liaisons between the two.

Salary range: $100,000 to $120,000.

Report to: IT Manager and User experience manager

Who We're Seeking:

Proven experience of at least 3 years as a User Experience (UX) Designer. You will have an empathy for users to better understand their perspectives and needs and the ability to advocate user needs and preferences throughout the design process.

Key responsibilities:

  1. Conducting user research to understand the target audience, their needs, and behaviors.
  2. Creating user personas to guide design decisions based on real user insights.
  3. Collaborating with cross-functional teams in generating and refining design concepts.
  4. Developing low-fidelity wireframes and interactive prototypes to visualize and communicate design ideas quickly.
  5. Collaborating with product managers, product owners, and developers to ensure a shared understanding of design goals, constraints, and priorities.

Required qualifications, skills, knowledge and attributes.

Essential skill

  • Should Have At Least 3 Years As A User Experience (Ux) Designer In Complex Enterprise Software Environments.
  • Attention To Details In Design Elements, Interactions, And User Interfaces.
  • Understanding Of The Technical Aspects Of Software Development To Understand Feasibility Of Design And To Communicate To Developers.
  • Ability To Analyze Data And User Feedback To Derive Actionable Insights.
  • Excellent Problem-Solving Skills In Identifying Creative Solutions To Complex Problems.

Preferable skill
